PROJECT CHARTER
Project: Development and Implementation of Community Engagement Framework
Report Date: 2026-06-15

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This project aims to develop and implement a department-wide community engagement framework for the Philadelphia Police Department. The framework will include a community policing directive and standard operating procedures to enhance community trust and collaboration. By fostering consistent engagement practices, the project seeks to improve public safety outcomes and strengthen relationships between the police and the communities they serve.

STRATEGIC OBJECTIVE
Advance Trust with the Community and Partners

PROJECT PURPOSE
The purpose of this project is to establish a structured approach to community engagement that promotes transparency, inclusivity, and responsiveness to community needs. It aims to ensure that all districts within the Philadelphia Police Department adhere to a unified framework for engaging with community stakeholders.

KEY DELIVERABLES
- Community engagement framework document
- Standard operating procedures for community policing
- Training materials for officers on community engagement
- Implementation plan for district-level support
- Evaluation metrics for community engagement effectiveness

HIGH LEVEL REQUIREMENTS
- Development of a comprehensive community policing directive
- Creation of clear standard operating procedures
- Training sessions for all officers on the new framework
- Establishment of a feedback mechanism for community input
- Regular assessment of community engagement initiatives

PROJECT CONSTRAINTS
- Limited budget for training and resources
- Potential resistance to change within the department
- Time constraints for implementation across all districts
- Need for alignment with existing departmental policies
- Variability in community needs across different districts

PROJECT ASSUMPTIONS
- Community members are willing to engage with the police department
- Officers will participate in training and adopt new practices
- Sufficient resources will be allocated for implementation
- Stakeholders will provide constructive feedback during the process
- The framework will be adaptable to changing community dynamics

SCHEDULE MILESTONES
- Framework development completion: August 15
- Training program rollout: September 30
- Initial implementation in pilot districts: October 15
- Full implementation across all districts: December 15
- First evaluation of community engagement effectiveness: March 15

SUCCESS CRITERIA
- Increased community participation in engagement activities
- Positive feedback from community surveys regarding police interactions
- Reduction in reported incidents of community-police tensions
- Improved officer satisfaction with engagement processes
- Achievement of established performance metrics for community engagement

HIGH LEVEL RISKS
- Lack of community interest in engagement initiatives
- Insufficient training leading to inconsistent implementation
- Potential backlash from community members resistant to police presence
- Changes in leadership affecting project continuity
- Inadequate resources to support ongoing engagement efforts
