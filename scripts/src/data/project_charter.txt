PROJECT CHARTER
Project: Department-wide Community Engagement Framework Development
Report Date: 2026-06-16

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This project aims to develop and implement a comprehensive community engagement framework for the Philadelphia Police Department. The framework will establish clear standard operating procedures and ongoing support for district-level community policing efforts. By fostering stronger relationships with community stakeholders, the Department seeks to enhance trust and collaboration.

STRATEGIC OBJECTIVE
Advance Trust with the Community and Partners

PROJECT PURPOSE
The purpose of this project is to create a structured approach to community engagement that promotes transparency, responsiveness, and inclusivity in police-community interactions. This will help build lasting relationships and improve public safety outcomes.

KEY DELIVERABLES
- Community engagement framework document
- Standard operating procedures for community policing
- Training materials for district-level officers
- Implementation timeline and action plan
- Feedback mechanisms for community input

HIGH LEVEL REQUIREMENTS
- Involvement of community stakeholders in the framework development
- Alignment with departmental goals and community needs
- Training for officers on new engagement practices
- Establishment of metrics for evaluating engagement effectiveness

PROJECT CONSTRAINTS
- Limited resources for training and implementation
- Potential resistance from officers to new procedures
- Time constraints for community feedback and adjustments

PROJECT ASSUMPTIONS
- Community stakeholders will be willing to participate in the process
- Sufficient funding will be allocated for training and resources
- Officers will embrace the new community engagement strategies

SCHEDULE MILESTONES
- Framework draft completion: August 15
- Stakeholder review and feedback: September 30
- Final framework approval: October 15
- Training sessions for officers: November 1 - November 30
- Implementation start date: December 1

SUCCESS CRITERIA
- Increased community participation in engagement activities
- Positive feedback from community surveys on police transparency
- Reduction in community complaints related to police interactions
- Improved officer engagement scores in departmental surveys

HIGH LEVEL RISKS
- Failure to engage community stakeholders effectively
- Inadequate training leading to inconsistent implementation
- Negative public perception if initial rollout is poorly received

BUDGET
To be defined.
