PROJECT CHARTER
Project: Department-Wide Community Engagement Framework Development
Report Date: 2026-06-13

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This project aims to develop and implement a comprehensive community engagement framework for the Philadelphia Police Department. The framework will establish clear standard operating procedures and ongoing support to enhance community policing efforts across all districts. By fostering trust and collaboration, the project seeks to improve public safety and community relations.

STRATEGIC OBJECTIVE
Advance Trust with the Community and Partners

PROJECT PURPOSE
The purpose of this project is to create a structured approach to community engagement that ensures consistency and effectiveness in police-community interactions. This will empower officers to build trust and address community needs through collaborative efforts.

KEY DELIVERABLES
- Community engagement framework document
- Standard operating procedures for community policing
- Training materials for officers on community engagement
- Implementation timeline and milestones
- Evaluation metrics for community engagement effectiveness

HIGH LEVEL REQUIREMENTS
- Involvement of community stakeholders in the development process
- Training for all officers on the new framework
- Regular feedback mechanisms from community members
- Alignment with departmental goals and community needs

PROJECT CONSTRAINTS
- Limited budget for training and resources
- Potential resistance to change within the department
- Time constraints for implementation within the strategic plan timeline

PROJECT ASSUMPTIONS
- Community members will actively participate in the development process
- Officers will be receptive to new training and procedures
- Sufficient resources will be allocated for implementation

SCHEDULE MILESTONES
- Framework draft completion: August 15
- Stakeholder review and feedback: September 30
- Final framework approval: October 15
- Training sessions for officers: November 1 - December 15
- Implementation start date: January 5

SUCCESS CRITERIA
- Increased community participation in engagement activities
- Positive feedback from community surveys on police interactions
- Improved metrics on community trust and safety perceptions
- Successful completion of training for all officers

HIGH LEVEL RISKS
- Lack of community engagement leading to ineffective framework
- Insufficient training leading to inconsistent implementation
- Changes in leadership affecting project continuity

To be defined.
