PROJECT CHARTER
Project: Develop and Implement a Department-Wide Community Engagement Framework
Report Date: 2026-06-11

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This project aims to establish a comprehensive community engagement framework for the Philadelphia Police Department. By integrating a community policing directive and standard operating procedures, the project seeks to enhance trust and collaboration between the police and the communities they serve. The initiative will ensure ongoing support and guidance at the district level, fostering a more responsive and accountable police force.

STRATEGIC OBJECTIVE
Advance Trust with the Community and Partners

PROJECT PURPOSE
The purpose of this project is to create a structured approach to community engagement that promotes transparency, accountability, and collaboration. This will ultimately lead to improved public safety outcomes and stronger community relationships.

KEY DELIVERABLES
- Community engagement framework document
- Standard operating procedures for community policing
- Training materials for district-level personnel
- Implementation timeline and milestones
- Evaluation and feedback mechanisms

HIGH LEVEL REQUIREMENTS
- Engagement framework must be inclusive and accessible to all community members
- Standard operating procedures must align with department-wide goals
- Training programs must be developed for all ranks within the department
- Regular assessments of community needs and feedback must be established

PROJECT CONSTRAINTS
- Limited budget for training and implementation
- Potential resistance from department personnel to new procedures
- Need for alignment with existing departmental policies and practices

PROJECT ASSUMPTIONS
- Community members will actively participate in engagement initiatives
- Department personnel will support the implementation of new procedures
- Sufficient resources will be allocated for training and development

SCHEDULE MILESTONES
- Framework draft completion: August 15
- Training program rollout: October 1
- Initial community feedback session: December 5
- Final framework implementation: February 20

SUCCESS CRITERIA
- Increased community participation in engagement activities
- Positive feedback from community surveys regarding police transparency
- Improved trust metrics in community-police relationships

HIGH LEVEL RISKS
- Community engagement efforts may not yield expected participation
- Resistance to change within the department could hinder implementation
- Insufficient resources may limit the effectiveness of the framework

BUDGET
To be defined.
