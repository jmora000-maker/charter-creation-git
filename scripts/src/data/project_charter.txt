PROJECT CHARTER
Project: Develop and Implement a Department-Wide Community Engagement Framework
Report Date: 2026-06-15

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This project aims to develop and implement a comprehensive community engagement framework for the Philadelphia Police Department. By establishing clear standard operating procedures and ongoing support, the project will enhance community trust and ensure consistent engagement across all districts. The initiative is designed to align with the Department's strategic objective of advancing trust with the community and partners.

STRATEGIC OBJECTIVE
Advance Trust with the Community and Partners

PROJECT PURPOSE
The purpose of this project is to create a structured approach to community engagement that fosters trust and collaboration between the Philadelphia Police Department and the communities it serves. This will involve developing guidelines and support systems to ensure effective communication and engagement.

KEY DELIVERABLES
- Community engagement framework document
- Standard operating procedures for community engagement
- Training materials for district officers
- Implementation plan with timelines and responsibilities
- Evaluation metrics for community engagement effectiveness

HIGH LEVEL REQUIREMENTS
- Involvement of community stakeholders in the development process
- Training for officers on community engagement practices
- Regular updates and feedback mechanisms for continuous improvement
- Integration of community feedback into departmental policies
- Collaboration with existing community organizations

PROJECT CONSTRAINTS
- Limited budget for training and implementation
- Potential resistance from officers to new engagement practices
- Time constraints for developing and rolling out the framework
- Need for alignment with existing departmental policies
- Variability in community needs and expectations

PROJECT ASSUMPTIONS
- Community members will be willing to participate in the engagement process
- Officers will be receptive to training and new procedures
- Sufficient resources will be allocated for project implementation
- Community engagement will lead to improved trust and collaboration
- Stakeholders will provide constructive feedback during development

SCHEDULE MILESTONES
- Framework development completion - August 15
- Training program rollout - September 30
- Initial community feedback session - October 15
- Implementation of standard operating procedures - November 30
- Evaluation of engagement effectiveness - June 15, 2027

SUCCESS CRITERIA
- Increased community participation in engagement activities
- Positive feedback from community surveys regarding police engagement
- Improved trust metrics in community-police relations
- Successful implementation of standard operating procedures
- Reduction in community complaints related to police interactions

HIGH LEVEL RISKS
- Lack of community interest in engagement initiatives
- Insufficient training leading to ineffective implementation
- Changes in leadership affecting project continuity
- Negative media coverage impacting community trust
- Inadequate resources to support ongoing engagement efforts
